About Me

As a child I always had 3 dream jobs - actress, author, or veterinarian. Once I got to college it became woefully apparent that I did not have the talent to pursue acting professionally and given the familial pressure to have a stable, 'real' job (ergo, not being a writer) I ultimately decided to pursue veterinary medicine, believing in my youthful naïveté that I would be able to write in my spare time.

Thus began the challenging road to becoming a veterinarian - a plethora of pre-requisites, perfect grades, test scores, and hundreds of hours of experience in a clinic were required before even applying to vet school. The application process was a year long but I managed to get in. After 4 years of grueling study and an 8 hour licensing exam, I had accomplished my goal of becoming a doctor.

I'll save the details of the trauma and pain that ensued once I started working for my therapy sessions but suffice it to say the job proved to be soul-wrenching, heart-and-back-breaking work. While it undoubtedly took several years off my life, I had to learn very quickly how to effectively communicate with my team and clients, how to handle emotionally charged conflicts and disputes, how to manage my time effectively and prioritize tasks, and manage my stress, all of which are important skills to learn not just for future jobs but for life in general.

After a particularly harrowing phone call with a client, I half-jokingly asked my colleague what I should do. He proceeded to tell me about a friend of his who went to a coding bootcamp and is now happily employed as a software engineer. My interest piqued, I began to research the world of software engineering. I bought several books on the subject, did a few free online courses and found that I really enjoyed it. It was mentally challenging, just like medicine, but without the physical and emotional challenges. Software engineering also allows for me to be creative, which I didn't realize was something I wanted until I started coding. I signed up for App Academy where I learned the fundamentals of software engineering.

I am now excited to join the tech field as a fully fledged software engineer and hope to use my knowledge of both coding and medicine to improve medical technologies and software for my fellow doctors!
